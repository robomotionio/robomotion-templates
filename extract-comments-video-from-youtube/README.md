# YouTube Video Comments

YouTube video comments are a goldmine of audience feedback - viewers share their honest reactions, ask questions, debate opinions, and leave feature requests right below the content. But YouTube's comment section has no bulk export, the Data API has strict quotas, and scrolling through thousands of comments manually is not feasible.

## What it extracts

- **Comment Text** — The full text of the YouTube comment.
- **Author** — The display name of the commenter.
- **Likes** — The number of likes the comment has received.
- **Reply Count** — How many replies are nested under the comment.
- **Timestamp** — When the comment was posted.
- **Author Channel URL** — Link to the commenter's YouTube channel.

## What you can do with it

- Bulk access to every comment on a video - text, likes, replies, and timestamps - exported as structured rows instead of endless scrolling.
- Audience sentiment analysis at scale: understand how viewers react to specific content, products mentioned, or topics discussed in videos.
- Creator feedback aggregation that turns scattered YouTube comments into organized data for identifying recurring themes, questions, and suggestions.
- Competitive video intelligence by analyzing the comment sections of competitor content to see what their audience cares about and complains about.

Enter the requested input when prompted. The flow opens the target page, extracts the fields listed above, and saves them as a CSV in your home folder.
