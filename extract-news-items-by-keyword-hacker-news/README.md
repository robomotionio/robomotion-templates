# Hacker News Keyword Search

Hacker News - Y Combinator's community bulletin board - is where technologists, startup founders, and engineers surface the content that shapes tech industry thinking. A front-page Hacker News post can drive thousands of visitors to a startup, spark industry debates, or signal emerging technology trends days before mainstream media catches on. Searching Hacker News by keyword reveals how the tech community discusses specific technologies, companies, and ideas - but the site's minimalist interface makes it difficult to extract that data for analysis.

## What it extracts

- **Position** — Ranking position of the post in search results.
- **News Title** — Title of the Hacker News submission.
- **News Source** — The original source or domain of the linked content.
- **Date** — When the post was submitted to Hacker News.
- **News Link** — Link to the original content being discussed.
- **Total Comments** — Number of discussion comments on the post.

## What you can do with it

- Brand mention monitoring: Track every time your company or product is discussed on Hacker News. Sentiment from this community heavily influences tech-savvy early adopters.
- Technology pulse reading: Filter for specific technologies (e.g., 'Rust', 'WebAssembly', 'LLM') to track discussion volume, sentiment, and trending perspectives over time.
- Competitor intelligence: Monitor Hacker News discussions about competitor products. High-scoring posts with many comments indicate significant community interest or concern.
- Content discovery: Find high-quality technical content and discussions about your focus areas. The community's voting system surfaces genuinely valuable links.

## How it works

Enter the requested input when prompted. The flow opens the target page, extracts the fields listed above, and saves them as a CSV in your home folder.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can I search for multiple keywords?**

Build your search query on Hacker News with multiple terms, Boolean operators, or quoted phrases before copying the URL. The robot extracts whatever the search returns.

**How is the score calculated?**

HN scores reflect net upvotes from the community. Higher scores indicate broader agreement that the post is interesting or valuable.

**Can I filter by date range?**

Yes. Hacker News search supports date range filtering. Apply your date filter before copying the URL and the robot extracts only results within that range.

**Does this capture 'Show HN' and 'Ask HN' posts?**

Yes. All post types appear in search results - stories, Show HN, Ask HN, and job posts - if they match your keyword.
