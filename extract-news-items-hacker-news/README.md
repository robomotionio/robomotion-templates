# Hacker News Front Page

The Hacker News front page is arguably the most influential curation of technology news on the internet. A post that reaches the front page has been upvoted by thousands of technologists - software engineers, startup founders, venture capitalists, and researchers - who collectively decide what matters in tech on any given day. The front page turns over several times daily, and missing a post means missing the conversation.

## What it extracts

- **Position** — Rank position on the Hacker News front page (#1, #2, #3, etc.).
- **News Title** — Title of the Hacker News submission.
- **News Link** — The website URL the post links to.
- **Source** — The domain of the website being linked to.

## What you can do with it

- Daily tech pulse capture: Extract the front page at the same time each day to create a consistent record of what the tech community considers most important.
- Source domain analysis: See which websites consistently reach the HN front page - personal blogs, major publications, GitHub repos, or startup announcements.
- Engagement benchmarking: Score and comment counts tell you how much discussion a topic generates. Track which themes drive the deepest conversations.
- Trend archiving: The front page is ephemeral. Scheduled extractions preserve what appeared and when, creating an analyzable timeline of tech discourse.

## How it works

Just run the flow. It loads the source, extracts the fields listed above, and saves them as a CSV in your home folder.

## Frequently asked questions

**How is this different from the keyword search scraper?**

This robot captures the current front page - whatever is trending right now. The keyword scraper finds historical posts matching specific search terms.

**How often does the front page change?**

The HN front page evolves continuously as posts gain and lose votes. Extracting 2-3 times per day captures the major cycles.

**Can I extract the 'Best' or 'Show HN' pages too?**

Yes. Navigate to any HN listing page - front page, best, new, show, ask - and copy the URL. The robot extracts whatever page you provide.

**Does it capture the post URLs?**

Yes. The robot extracts both the source URL (where the story links to) and the Hacker News discussion URL for each post.
