# ProBlogger Job Board

ProBlogger's job board has been connecting content creators with paying clients since the earliest days of professional blogging. Founded by Darren Rowse in 2004, the platform has evolved alongside the content industry - from blog posts and articles to content strategy, copywriting, social media management, technical writing, and multimedia content production. What sets ProBlogger apart is that every listing is specifically about content creation.

## What it extracts

- **Position** — The sequential position number of the listing.
- **Job Title** — The content position title.
- **Company Name** — The hiring organization or individual.
- **Location** — The geographic location or remote status of the role.
- **Job Type** — Freelance, contract, part-time, or full-time work arrangement.
- **Category** — The type of content needed (blog, email, technical, social, etc.).
- **Date Posted** — When the listing was published on ProBlogger.
- **Logo** — The company or client's logo image.
- **Job Link** — Direct URL to the full job listing on ProBlogger.

## What you can do with it

- Writing and content jobs exclusively - every listing is about content creation, editing, or content strategy. Zero noise from unrelated industries.
- Compensation visibility: ProBlogger listings often include pay rates, whether per-article, per-word, monthly retainer, or salary range. Extract this data for rate benchmarking.
- Content type specificity: listings specify what kind of content is needed - blog posts, whitepapers, social media copy, email newsletters, technical documentation, and more.
- Two decades of trust: ProBlogger's long history means established clients post here. The quality of opportunities is generally higher than general freelance platforms.
